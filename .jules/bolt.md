## 2025-05-25 - Redundant String Split Options and LINQ Multi-Enumeration
**Learning:** `StringSplitOptions.TrimEntries` and `StringSplitOptions.RemoveEmptyEntries` natively perform trimming and empty-check operations faster than appending `.Select(t => t.Trim()).Where(...)`. Additionally, `IEnumerable`s produced by `.Where` evaluate on every `.Count()` and `.ToList()` call, which in list endpoints (`ListTypes`, `ListNamespaces`) caused multiple iterations over large collections.
**Action:** Always check if `String.Split` options already cover the desired formatting to avoid redundant LINQ allocations. When chaining multiple operations (Count, Skip/Take, Select) on a dynamically filtered `IEnumerable`, materialize the filtered results with `.ToList()` early to prevent multi-enumeration.
